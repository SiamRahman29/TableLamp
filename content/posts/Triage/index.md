Here's the most common ethical dilemma. You hold a lever that controls train tracks and there are two tracks in front. On one track there is 1 young person tied and on the other an old person. You have to pull the lever to control which track the train takes.

Despite having a ghastly premise, many of us have heard and pondered some variation of this dilemma. Every time I have thought about this question, I pondered more about the point of the question: why think about this when there is no right answer especially when this scenario would never occur in real life. I was wrong. 

Many people, especially in healthcare and emergency response, face these situations in their lives. Situations where they are forced to make decisions on life and death, situations where they are forced to "triage" or prioritize who gets medicines/evacuations/help first.
